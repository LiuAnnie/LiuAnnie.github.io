Assignment 1: Blink!

The Goal

My goal is to blink 5 LEDs, one after the other. Each LED will independently blink on for 100 milliseconds and then off for 100 milliseconds, before the next LED blinks. I'll arrange my LEDs in a row on my breadboard and program the LEDs to blink in a back-and-forth pattern.



The Schematic

In my schematic, I have 5 differently colored LEDs connected to pins 9 through 13 on the Arduino. Using Ohm's Law, V=IR, I calculated the resistance needed so that 20 mA of current would flow through each LED. Since white and blue LEDs cause a greater voltage drop than red, yellow, and green LEDs, I calculated these resistor values separately. I found that the white and blue LEDs would need a 85 ohm resistor, and the red, yellow, and green LEDs would need a 160 ohm resistor. Since I don't have resistors of these values, I replaced the 85 ohm resistors with 100 ohm resistors and the 160 ohm resistors with 220 ohm resistors in my circuit.
